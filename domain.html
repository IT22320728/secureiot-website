Literature Survey

Existing IoT security approaches often address only specific layers of protection, such as static access control, gateway-only filtering, or isolated anomaly detection. This project investigates how Zero Trust, SDN, trust scoring, and deception-driven security can be combined into a unified and lightweight defense framework.

Research Gap

Many current solutions are too complex, costly, or resource-heavy for SOHO environments. A key gap exists in low-cost frameworks that can continuously verify devices, detect suspicious behaviour, dynamically enforce security policies, and learn from attacks through honeypot-driven intelligence.

Research Problem

IoT devices in SOHO environments are highly heterogeneous, resource-constrained, and often poorly managed. This makes them vulnerable to identity spoofing, lateral movement, malicious traffic injection, botnet recruitment, and service disruption. Traditional perimeter-based security is not sufficient to address these evolving threats.

Research Objectives

  • Design a lightweight Zero Trust architecture for IoT networks.
  • Authenticate devices using certificate-based identity management.
  • Apply SDN for adaptive and centralized access policy control.
  • Detect suspicious behaviour using trust and anomaly analysis.
  • Integrate honeypots for threat diversion and intelligence gathering.

Methodology

  • Build a layered architecture with management, control, gateway, and device layers.
  • Use Raspberry Pi and Ryu as the programmable SDN security controller environment.
  • Collect behavioural and flow data for threat detection and trust evaluation.
  • Apply dynamic policy enforcement based on risk level and device trust.
  • Redirect suspicious traffic to honeypots and extract insights for defense improvement.
